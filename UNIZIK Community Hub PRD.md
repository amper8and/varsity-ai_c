# UNIZIK Community Hub PRD

## Document Summary

- Product: UNIZIK Community Hub
- Client context: AIM concept for Nnamdi Azikiwe University community monetization and lifelong affinity
- Platform shape: Mobile-first community hub with AI concierge, commerce, campus utility, and alumni engagement
- Draft date: 2026-03-14
- Status: Draft for concept validation

## 1. Opportunity

Nnamdi Azikiwe University already has the ingredients of a high-value community platform: a large and competitive student body, a broad faculty footprint, multiple campuses, active institutional communications, and a long-lived alumni network. The existing AIM and MTN concept decks show a repeatable commercial pattern: combine verified university identity, useful services, partner offers, and AI assistance in one experience so community spending and engagement can be channeled through a university-owned hub.

The gap is experience quality. Most university digital touchpoints feel fragmented, administrative, and web-first. The opportunity for UNIZIK is to present a cleaner, more modern mobile hub that feels less like a portal and more like a trusted community product: useful every week, not just at admission time.

## 2. Product Vision

UNIZIK Community Hub is a mobile app that helps students and alumni access trusted campus information, discover useful offers and services, build career relationships, and interact with an AI concierge grounded in university context, while creating new non-tuition revenue for the university through partner campaigns, transactions, and premium services.

## 3. Strategic Goals

1. Increase the lifetime engagement of students and alumni with the university.
2. Create a new university-owned demand channel for partner brands and service providers.
3. Improve discoverability of student and alumni services across campuses and faculties.
4. Make the UNIZIK brand feel more modern, useful, and digitally connected.
5. Prove a commercially viable model that can scale beyond a showcase app into an operational platform.

## 4. Problem Statement

Students and alumni currently experience university life through disconnected systems, informal WhatsApp groups, third-party listings, and inconsistent information sources. That fragmentation creates three problems:

1. Community members miss trusted opportunities, services, and benefits.
2. The university under-monetizes the spending power and loyalty of its community.
3. Brands and service providers lack a credible, permissioned way to reach the UNIZIK audience with relevance.

## 7. Product Positioning

UNIZIK Community Hub is not just a campus app, not just an alumni app, and not just an offers marketplace.

It is a verified university community super-app with four integrated value pillars:

1. Utility: trusted university information and services
2. Commerce: relevant offers, bundles, and partner marketplace
3. Connection: events, groups, mentorship, and alumni network
4. Intelligence: an AI concierge that helps users navigate all of the above

## 8. Design Principles For The Product

1. Useful before impressive. Every surface should solve a real weekly need.
2. Community before campaigns. Promotions must feel earned and relevant.
3. Verified trust. Identity, offers, and information should clearly signal credibility.
4. Mobile-native simplicity. The product should feel effortless on low attention and low bandwidth.
5. Lifelong relevance. The experience should evolve from student life to alumni life.
6. Segmented personalization. Faculty, campus, life stage, and interests should shape the feed.

## 9. Context From Reviewed Material

The existing decks point to a stable core concept that should carry into the UNIZIK version:

- Community monetization through partner promotions, transactions, and service subscriptions
- AI-assisted navigation of services and recommendations
- Shared-value model among university, platform operator, and commercial partners
- Strong value proposition in categories such as accommodation, transport, groceries, career services, tutoring, and mobile bundles
- A university experience layer that supplies context to the AI assistant and marketplace

The revised UNIZIK concept should keep that model but make the consumer experience feel more cohesive, modern, and emotionally resonant.

## 10. Proposed MVP Scope

### 10.1 Must-Have MVP Modules

#### A. Identity and Onboarding

- Sign up with email, phone, or student/alumni identifier
- Verification states: student, alumni, staff, guest
- Capture faculty, department, campus, graduation year, interests
- Consent collection for communications and partner offers

#### B. Home Hub

- Personalized feed
- Quick actions for top tasks
- Featured offers
- Upcoming events
- Recommended communities or mentors
- AI prompt suggestions

#### C. AI Concierge

- Ask about campus info, services, events, opportunities, and offers
- Recommend next best actions
- Guide users to relevant partners or university resources
- Support natural-language search for marketplace and community content

#### D. Deals and Marketplace

- Featured offers and discount cards
- Service categories such as food, transport, data bundles, accommodation, health, fashion, learning, jobs
- Merchant detail pages
- Save, redeem, inquire, or buy flows
- Verified partner badge and offer expiry handling

#### E. Community

- Events feed
- Interest-based groups or chapters
- Alumni spotlight stories
- Mentorship interest matching
- Community posts in a moderated format

#### F. Career and Growth

- Jobs and internships
- Mentor directory
- Business directory for alumni-owned businesses
- Skill-building recommendations and premium micro-services later

#### G. Wallet, Rewards, and Referrals

- Offer redemptions
- Reward points or badges
- Referral tracking for user growth
- Membership and verification status visibility

### 10.2 Admin MVP

- Partner onboarding and offer management
- Campaign targeting by segment
- Event publishing
- Basic moderation tools
- Analytics dashboard for reach, engagement, redemption, and conversion

## 11. Out Of Scope For MVP

- Full student records system replacement
- End-to-end academic administration
- Broad public social network features
- Peer-to-peer payments
- Complex live chat support team
- Full e-commerce logistics stack

## 12. Recommended User Journeys

### Journey 1: New Student Activation

1. Student installs app after admission or matriculation.
2. Student verifies identity and selects campus, faculty, and interests.
3. Home feed shows orientation help, transport deals, mobile bundles, accommodation help, and key dates.
4. Student asks AI concierge a question such as where to get verified accommodation near campus.
5. Student saves an offer and joins a faculty or interest group.

### Journey 2: Alumni Reconnection

1. Alumni joins with graduation year and faculty.
2. App surfaces alumni stories, networking events, business listings, and mentorship opportunities.
3. Alumni opts into mentoring or posts a business listing.
4. Alumni receives relevant professional offers and event invitations.

### Journey 3: Partner Campaign Conversion

1. Partner creates a targeted campaign for a segment such as engineering students in Awka.
2. Offer appears in feed, search, and contextual AI recommendations.
3. User clicks through, redeems, or purchases.
4. Platform reports conversion and attributable revenue.

## 14. Functional Requirements

### Account and Identity

- Support verified role assignment
- Support progressive profiling
- Support role-based content access

### Personalization

- Segment by role, campus, faculty, department, graduation year, geography, and interests
- Personalize home feed and campaign targeting

### Content and Campaigns

- Manage editorial cards, banners, stories, and announcements
- Publish targeted partner campaigns
- Schedule event promotions and reminders

### Search and Discovery

- Unified search across offers, events, businesses, mentors, and help content
- Autocomplete and suggested queries
- Filters with persistent applied states

### AI Layer

- Retrieval from approved university and platform content
- Citation or source attribution for critical answers where possible
- Guardrails against unsupported academic or regulatory claims
- Escalation path to official pages or human contact points

### Commerce and Conversion

- Offer redemption state
- Click, lead, coupon, and transaction tracking
- Partner attribution and reporting

### Community and Safety

- Moderation queue
- Reporting and block tools
- Verified badges for official accounts and approved merchants

### Analytics

- MAU, DAU, activation, retention
- Offer views, saves, redemptions, conversions
- Event RSVPs and attendance proxies
- AI engagement and resolution rate
- Segment-level engagement insights

## 15. Non-Functional Requirements

- Mobile-first and responsive
- Fast on mid-range Android devices
- Bandwidth-sensitive image and content handling
- Secure authentication and consent logging
- Audit trail for campaigns and moderation
- Accessible typography and color contrast
- Scalable content model for multi-campus expansion

## 16. Commercial Model

Following the AIM/MTN concept logic, the revenue engine should combine multiple streams so the product is not dependent on one monetization mechanic.

### Priority Revenue Streams

1. Partner campaign fees
2. Transaction commissions on marketplace purchases or lead conversions
3. Sponsored placements in Explore and Home
4. Mobile or connectivity bundles
5. Premium service subscriptions or AI agents for selected use cases
6. Business-directory upgrades for alumni merchants

### Commercial Rule

Promotions should only appear when they are relevant to the user segment and additive to the community experience. The platform should feel like a member-benefit product, not an ad container.

## 17. Launch Strategy

### Phase 0: Concept Validation

- Validate demand with students, alumni, and university stakeholders
- Confirm high-intent categories
- Identify first launch partners

### Phase 1: Showcase MVP

- Deliver polished mobile prototype or live MVP
- Focus on Home, AI, Deals, Events, and Profile
- Use a curated set of trusted offers and editorial content

### Phase 2: Operational Pilot

- Launch verified accounts
- Add partner self-service and analytics
- Introduce mentorship, jobs, and business directory

### Phase 3: Revenue Scale

- Broaden partner network
- Add wallet, rewards, referrals, and premium services
- Expand into parents and friends-and-family segments

## 19. Key Risks

1. Overloading the app with too many modules at launch
2. Letting promotions overpower trust and utility
3. Weak identity verification reducing partner confidence
4. Poor content freshness making the hub feel abandoned
5. AI hallucinations or outdated answers damaging credibility
6. Dependence on manual partner operations without a repeatable workflow

## 20. Mitigations

1. Launch with a focused MVP and limited categories
2. Separate official, editorial, and sponsored content clearly
3. Use staged verification and verified badges
4. Build lightweight content operations and publishing calendars
5. Limit AI answers to approved knowledge sources and route edge cases to official pages
6. Start with a small set of anchor partners and repeatable campaign templates

## 21. Open Questions

1. Will the first release be a clickable showcase, a prototype-backed concept app, or a production pilot?
2. What university-owned systems can realistically be integrated in phase 1?
3. What approval process will govern commercial partners and sponsored content?
4. Should the first audience include only current students and alumni, or also staff and parents?
5. Will the AI assistant answer purely informational questions, or also handle transactions and booking flows?

## 22. Recommendation

For the first UNIZIK version, prioritize a showcase MVP that proves three things clearly:

1. Users trust the app because it feels officially connected to the university.
2. The app is genuinely useful because it solves everyday discovery and support problems.
3. The commercial model is credible because offers and partner services convert without degrading the experience.

If those three are demonstrated well, the concept becomes much easier to scale into a full community platform.
